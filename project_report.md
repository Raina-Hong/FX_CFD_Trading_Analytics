## Key Findings
解释结果
1. Gold generated the strongest after-cost cumulative return of approximately 79.7%, with a Sharpe ratio of 0.98.
2. AUD/USD performed poorly after costs, with a cumulative return of -31.6% and a negative Sharpe ratio.
3. NASDAQ100 had the highest total execution cost, mainly driven by slippage during volatile periods.
4. Transaction costs reduced returns across all assets, showing why execution quality matters in FX and CFD trading.
5. The moving-average strategy worked better on trending assets such as Gold, but was less effective on range-bound FX pairs.
